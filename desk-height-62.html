Ideal Desk Height for a 6’2” Tall Person Introduction If you’re 6’2”, many desks can still feel a bit too low. Most standard desks are made for average-height users, which can cause tall people to lean forward or raise their shoulders while working. Over time, this can lead to neck, back, and shoulder pain. Getting the right desk height helps you sit and work more comfortably throughout the day. The Best Desk Height for a 6’2” Person For someone who is 6’2”, the ideal desk height is usually: 28.5 to 30 inches from the floor to the top of the desk This height works best when: Your feet are flat on the floor Your knees are at about hip level Your elbows bend at about 90 degrees Your shoulders stay relaxed If you use a standing desk, a good standing height is usually: 43 to 45 inches These numbers are general guidelines. Your arm length, chair height, and posture can affect what feels best for you. Why Desk Height Matters Using the wrong desk height can cause discomfort over time: Neck pain from leaning forward Shoulder tension from raised arms Lower back pain from slouching Wrist strain from awkward typing angles A desk at the right height helps keep your body aligned and reduces daily fatigue. Common Mistakes Tall People Make Using a standard desk without checking the height Most desks are about 28 inches tall, which may still be too low for someone 6’2”. Raising the chair too much This can cause your feet to lose contact with the floor. Ignoring monitor height A low screen can cause neck strain even if your desk height is correct. Typing with bent wrists Wrists should stay straight while typing, not angled up or down. Simple Setup Tips Adjust your chair before your desk Keep your elbows close to your body Position your monitor at eye level Use a footrest if your feet don’t sit flat Frequently Asked Questions Is a standing desk helpful for tall people? Yes. Standing desks allow easy height adjustments and work well for tall users. Can I use a keyboard tray? Yes. A keyboard tray can help if your desk is slightly too high. What if my desk can’t be adjusted? Desk risers can raise it a few inches if needed. Does arm length matter? Yes. Taller people often have longer arms, which affects desk height comfort. Final Thoughts For a 6’2” tall person, the right desk height can improve comfort, posture, and focus. Small changes to your setup can make a big difference in how you feel during and after work.
