Ideal Desk Height for a 6’5” Tall Person Introduction If you’re 6’5”, most desks feel too low. Standard desks are built for average-height users, not tall people. This often leads to slouching, raised shoulders, and neck pain—especially if you work at a desk for many hours a day. The right desk height helps your body stay relaxed and supported while you work. The Best Desk Height for a 6’5” Person For someone who is 6’5”, the ideal desk height is usually: 30 to 32 inches from the floor to the top of the desk This height works best when: Your feet are flat on the floor Your elbows bend at about 90 degrees Your shoulders stay relaxed Your wrists stay straight while typing If you use a standing desk, a good standing height is usually: 45 to 47 inches These are general guidelines. Arm length, chair height, and posture can slightly change what feels best for you. Why Desk Height Matters A desk that’s too low or too high can cause problems over time: Back pain from slouching Neck strain from leaning forward Shoulder tension from raised arms Wrist pain from awkward typing angles When your desk is at the right height, your body stays in a natural position, which reduces pain and fatigue. Common Mistakes Tall People Make Using a standard desk Most desks are about 28–29 inches tall, which is often too low for someone 6’5”. Raising the chair too high This can cause your feet to dangle and put pressure on your legs. Ignoring screen height If your monitor is too low, you’ll bend your neck even if the desk height is correct. Skipping arm support Lack of arm support can strain your shoulders over time. Simple Setup Tips Adjust your chair before adjusting your desk Keep elbows close to your body Place the top of your screen at eye level Use a footrest if needed Frequently Asked Questions Is a standing desk good for tall people? Yes. Standing desks are great for tall users because they adjust higher than standard desks. Can I fix a desk that’s too low? Yes. Desk risers or furniture lifts can raise your desk a few inches. Does desk depth matter? Yes. A deeper desk helps keep your monitor at a safe distance. Should my arms rest on the desk or armrests? Either is fine, as long as your shoulders stay relaxed. Final Thoughts For a 6’5” tall person, the right desk height can greatly improve comfort and posture. Small adjustments can help you work longer with less pain and strain.
